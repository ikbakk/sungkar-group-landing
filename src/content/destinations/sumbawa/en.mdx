description: >
  Sumbawa is a large island east of Lombok that remains relatively untouched by mass tourism, making it an ideal destination for more authentic adventures. Its nature is wild and diverse — from white-sand beaches, world-class surf breaks, and hidden waterfalls to interactions with whale sharks in their natural habitat.

  Saleh Bay in northern Sumbawa is one of the best spots in Indonesia for snorkeling with whale sharks. Unlike other locations, whale sharks here appear naturally — not because of feeding — making the experience more ethical and sustainable.

  For surfers, Lakey Peak in southern Sumbawa is legendary. Its consistently long waves draw surfers from around the world. If you prefer land-based exploration, Moyo Island offers the exotic Mata Jitu Waterfall, while Tambora holds the story of the devastating 1815 eruption.
