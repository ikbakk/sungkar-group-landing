description: >
  Tanjung Ringgit is a peninsula at the southeastern tip of Lombok offering dramatic limestone cliffs and an endless open ocean. It is one of Lombok's most photogenic spots, with white cliffs towering above deep blue waters.

  Beyond its natural panorama, Tanjung Ringgit holds historical value. The area has caves and cannons from the Japanese occupation during World War II that can be explored. Its name — "ringgit" — is said to come from the cliff shapes resembling ringgit coins.

  Tanjung Ringgit is often combined with Pink Beach and Gili Pasir tours. Its location near Tanjung Luar Harbor makes it a strategic starting point for boat trips to nearby islands. Sunrise here is also spectacular — the cliffs bathed in the day's first golden light.
