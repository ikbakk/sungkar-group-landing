description: >
  Sade Traditional Village is a traditional Sasak settlement near Kuta Lombok. This village serves as an authentic window into the life, architecture, and traditions of Lombok's indigenous people that have survived for hundreds of years.

  The houses in Sade Village are called "bale tani" — buildings with woven bamboo walls (bedek), thatched roofs, and floors coated with a mixture of clay and buffalo dung. This floor technique, called "semen abu," is believed to repel insects and keep the house cool. Each house has a specific function — there's a bale dalam (sleeping room), bale luar (guest room), and rice barn.

  Village tours are guided by local residents who share the history, customs, and life philosophy of the Sasak people. Visitors can also watch traditional weaving demonstrations and buy handicrafts directly from artisans. The tour typically ends with a photo session against the backdrop of traditional houses and authentic village life.
